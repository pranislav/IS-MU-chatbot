Manual to export Course Opinion Poll results

  • 1.Path to the Course Opinion Poll results application

    Teacher:

    Information system Teacher course selection Course Opinion Poll

    Student:

    Information system Student End of term Course opinion polls

    Top

  • 2.Selection of courses

    First part of application – My courses – works the same for all user levels, only the assortment of options that the user sees differs. One can always display either all the objects selected by the option or click them in the drop-down list. The "Browse all the courses" option then differs only in the user's preselected faculties.

    Top

  • 3.Student's view
    1 – When entering the Course Opinion Poll results application, the student has preselected by default the subjects that he/she assessed in the given semester and that he/she is registered/enrolled in in the selected semester.
    2 – The option "My courses" after clicking "Select only certain courses" will show only the relevant objects (course–teacher or seminar group–tutor combinations) according to the selected period in the list (3).

    Top

  • 4.Teacher's view – selected courses that the person teaches
    4 – The teacher sees all courses to which he/she is assigned in the Course Catalogue. The lecturer sees all objects of the seminar tutors (and other lecturers) of the given course, including assigned verbal comments. The seminar tutor then sees all the objects of the course but can display the verbal comments only to those that are taught by him/her.

    Top

  • 5.View of teacher and guarantor at the same time
    5 – If the teacher is also a course guarantor, he/she can limit the selection of displayed courses in various ways. Only courses he/she teaches, only guaranteed courses or a combination of both can be chosen.

    Top

    6 – The teacher who is also the course guarantor can display all the courses taught or guaranteed in a given semester in one list. Course guarantor sees all verbal comments in the courses guaranteed by him/her.

    Top

  • 6.View of teacher with rights for Course Opinion Poll
    7 – The teacher may have the rights to display the results of the Course Opinion Poll for the parent unit (faculty, the whole university). This means an access to poll answers, including all text comments. The application then allows to choose to display only the courses that the person directly teaches or to display the results of all courses for which the person has the rights at once.
    8 – The person with the rights to the Course Opinion Poll can display a list (9) of all courses to which he/she has the rights (access to the poll answers, including text comments). In the example, a person has the rights to the Course Opinion Poll for the faculty (LF).

    Top

  • 7.View of teacher and guarantor at the same time with rights for Course Opinion Poll
    10 – The teacher, who is also the guarantor and has the rights to the Course Opinion Poll, has the most choices of course selection to the list for displaying their results. For example, it is possible to choose the list of courses taught and guaranteed at the same time.
    11 – A person, who is a teacher, guarantor and has the rights to the Course Opinion Poll, can list the courses (12) whose results (including text comments) he/she can read.

    Top

  • 8.Advanced course selection from the Catalogue
    13 – Clicking Advanced course selection from the Course Catalogue, which will show after clicking Browse all the courses, will display a pop-up window with additional filter options for the course selection.

    14 – On the right side of the window, there is a field at the top for entering the search phrase, whether it is for example in the courses' or persons' names. Beneath, there are extending attributes such as the course faculty, the term under which it was listed, or buttons for selecting courses taught or guaranteed by the person.

    After setting all the required filters, we click on the Show button (15), which will show a list of matching courses on the right side (16). We can select only some of them, or use the options below: mark all, mark all this page.

    Finally, we confirm everything by clicking Go to application.

    17 – The codes of the selected courses are now displayed near the Advanced course selection from the Course Catalogue link. The drop-down list below (18) shows all objects (course–teacher or seminar group–tutor combinations) of these courses.

    Top

  • 9.Results display options for teachers

    After the previous selection of the objects whose results we want to display, it is possible to set different types of their sorting and displaying.

    19 – When displaying the Course Opinion Poll results, it is possible to sort these results in different ways.

    Default sorting by courses displays results by first showing the complete results of one subject, followed by the results of other subjects.

    Sorting by questions shows results by first displaying the results of question 1 for all selected courses, then the results of question 2 for all selected courses, and so on. This sorting is convenient, for example, for superiors who want to compare the results of individual teachers according to parameters measured by the poll.

    Finally, sorting by respondents shows the results by listing individual particular answers of individual respondents, without the possibility of identifying the specific persons who filled in the poll (respondents are labelled only with a serial number). This view is useful, for example, to verify the consistency of individual student responses and their text comments within a single course or seminar.

    20 – The Course Opinion Poll results can be displayed complete (the "all" option), only closed questions (this is how students see the Course Opinion Poll results) or only open text comments without charts can be displayed.

    Top

  • 10.Print options

    The Course Opinion Poll results can be displayed not only in the browser but can also be printed with various print settings.
